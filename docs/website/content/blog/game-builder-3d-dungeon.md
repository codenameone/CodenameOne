![Build a 3D dungeon with the Game Builder](/blog/gamebuilder/dungeon-5-walk.png)

The final [Game Builder](/manual/game-builder/) tutorial — a **first-person 3D dungeon** ("Crypt Walk"). See [Tutorial 1 — 2D platformer](/blog/game-builder-2d-platformer/) and [Tutorial 2 — board game](/blog/game-builder-board-game/) for the basics. The in-editor preview is for quick iteration; on device the scene is rendered by the GPU-accelerated `GameSceneView`, which turns each element into a `Model` under a perspective camera with lighting.

Every screenshot is regenerated by the `BlogTutorialScreenshots` test; the steps are asserted runnable by `TutorialValidationHarness`.

## Getting started

From a **Java 17** Codename One project:

```bash
mvn cn1:create-game-scene -DclassName=com.example.game.CryptWalk -Dmode=3d
mvn cn1:gamebuilder
```

## Step 1 — New 3D scene

Choose **New scene → 3D Map**. The editor shows the board (placement defaults to an accurate top-down grid; toggle **View → 3D: Perspective / Top-down** for an angled overview). The **3D Kit** pack supplies blocks, pillars, crates, a spawn and scenery.

![New 3D scene with the 3D Kit](/blog/gamebuilder/dungeon-1-new-scene.png)

## Step 2 — Pick the play style

With nothing selected, set the Inspector's **3D play style** to **dungeon**. The options are *open* (a free arena), *flight* (an aerial fly-over), *race* (a road racer) and *dungeon* (first-person walls).

![Choose the dungeon play style](/blog/gamebuilder/dungeon-2-style.png)

## Step 3 — Build the crypt

From the **3D Kit**, place **Pillar** assets to form walls and a **Rock** as scenery. Use the Inspector's per-object **Size** to vary them — that scales only the selected object, not the shared asset.

![Build walls from pillars](/blog/gamebuilder/dungeon-3-walls.png)

## Step 4 — Add the player

Place a **Spawn** and tick **This is the player** in its Behavior section. Its **Elevation (Z)** field lets you raise it for multi-level layouts.

![Add the player spawn](/blog/gamebuilder/dungeon-4-spawn.png)

## Step 5 — Walk it

Press **Live**: in *dungeon* style you walk in **first person** — **Left/Right turn, Up/Down walk** — and the pillars act as walls you can't pass through. A radar in the corner shows where everything is. Switch the play style to *open* for a free arena or *flight* for an aerial fly-over of the same layout.

![Walk the dungeon in first person](/blog/gamebuilder/dungeon-5-walk.png)

## Bigger worlds

For terrain-driven 3D games, the **Terrain** tool sculpts ground height, carves holes, paints surface materials (grass/road/stone/…) and stamps walls — painted ramps render as smooth slopes. The runtime game-space model also supports **streaming, chunked terrain and loadable regions** for large/open worlds that exceed memory. Because a level is plain data, you can re-edit any of these later without touching your `onUpdate` logic.

That's the trilogy — give it a try with `mvn cn1:create-game-scene` then `mvn cn1:gamebuilder`.
